---

Purpose

This document defines the canonical audit checklist
for verifying governance integrity, authority boundaries,
and AI participation controls in stablecoin operations governed by Prospera OS.

It converts governance specifications into auditable verification points.

---

Audit Scope

This checklist applies to:

- Stablecoin issuance, redemption, and circulation
- Crisis and emergency governance
- AI participation and decision boundaries
- Kernel enforcement and execution control
- Data protection and regulatory compliance

---

Section A — Governance Authority Verification

A1. Governance authority is defined exclusively in Prospera OS governance layer.
A2. No governance rules are defined or modified within client repositories.
A3. Escalation paths are explicitly documented and enforced.
A4. Emergency governance does not bypass authority validation.

---

Section B — Decision Chain Integrity (Seven-Step Model)

B1. Intent declaration is human-authorized and documented.
B2. Risk and context assessment precedes all actions.
B3. Action boundaries are explicitly defined before authorization.
B4. Governance approval is recorded and traceable.
B5. Kernel enforcement is active and non-bypassable.
B6. Execution follows approved scope only.
B7. Post-action audit and accountability are completed.

---

Section C — Crisis and Emergency Controls

C1. Crisis classification criteria are defined and applied.
C2. Severity levels trigger predefined governance responses.
C3. Prohibited actions are explicitly blocked.
C4. Emergency actions remain reversible where legally possible.
C5. Crisis resolution includes mandatory post-event audit.

---

Section D — AI Participation Boundary

D1. AI is formally designated as an engineering worker only.
D2. AI cannot authorize, execute, or escalate decisions.
D3. AI-generated outputs are advisory and reviewed by humans.
D4. AI invocation is restricted to authorized processes.
D5. Kernel blocks any prohibited AI behavior.

---

Section E — Kernel Enforcement Verification

E1. Kernel enforces authority boundaries at runtime.
E2. Unauthorized execution paths are blocked.
E3. Privilege escalation is technically impossible.
E4. Emergency conditions do not relax kernel enforcement.

---

Section F — Auditability and Traceability

F1. All decisions are logged with timestamps.
F2. Responsible human actors are identified.
F3. AI-assisted activities are traceable.
F4. Audit records are retained according to policy.

---

Section G — Regulatory and Data Compliance

G1. Data usage complies with applicable regulations.
G2. Customer data access is controlled and logged.
G3. Cross-border data flows are governed.
G4. Regulatory disclosures follow approved procedures.

---

Audit Outcome Classification

- Compliant
- Compliant with Observations
- Non-Compliant (Remediation Required)
- Critical Governance Violation

---

Canonical Outcome

Successful completion of this checklist certifies
that stablecoin operations remain governed,
auditable, and defensible under Prospera OS.
